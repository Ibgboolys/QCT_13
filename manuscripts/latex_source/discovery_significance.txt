We present a comprehensive analysis of the recently discovered relationship $\Lambda_{\rm micro}/m_p^{\rm QCD} \approx \sqrt{2/3}$, where $\Lambda_{\rm micro} = \sqrt{E_{\rm pair} \times m_\nu}$ is the fundamental microscopic scale of Quantum Compression Theory (QCT). We demonstrate that this coupling:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Explains} proton stability via maximal condensate coupling
    \item \textbf{Drives} neutron $\beta$-decay through coupling energy gain
    \item \textbf{Predicts} universal baryon mass shifts $\delta m_B \propto f_B^2$ where $f_B = \sqrt{\langle Q^2 \rangle_B}$
    \item \textbf{Solves} the ``why is the proton mass 938 MeV?'' problem
    \item \textbf{Provides} a natural cutoff for vacuum energy, addressing the cosmological constant problem
\end{itemize}

If confirmed by lattice QCD and precision experiments, this discovery represents one of the most profound advances in fundamental physics since the completion of the Standard Model.
\end{abstract}

\begin{equation}
\frac{\Lambda_{\rm micro}}{m_p^{\rm QCD}} = \frac{733\,\text{MeV}}{929.3\,\text{MeV}} = 0.789
